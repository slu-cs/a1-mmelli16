How to Register to Vote

Qualifications to Register to Vote in St Lawrence County:

You must be a United States citizen;

You must be 18 years old by December 31st of the year in which you file your registration form (note - you must be 18 years old by the date of the general, primary, or other election in which you want to vote);

You must be 18 years old by December 31st of the year in which you file your registration form (note - you must be 18 years old by the date of the general, primary, or other election in which you want to vote);

You must be a resident of St. Lawrence County for at least 30 days prior to the election;

You must not be in prison or on parole for a felony conviction (unless parolee pardoned or restored rights of citizenship);

You must not be claiming the right to vote elsewhere.

Any duly registered voter is eligible to vote in a General Election, and in a Primary Election voters who are enrolled in the party that is holding the Primary are eligible.

Registering In Person

Registration forms are available at most government offices and post offices, or you may register in person Monday through Friday at the St. Lawrence County Board of Elections Office.

Choosing the Correct Form

When registering to vote via the DMV make sure you receive the correct form. One form allows you to register for the first time, the other form is used if you've moved within St. Lawrence County and wish to notify the Board of Elections of your new address to maintain active voting status. Also note it can take up to a month for registrations from the DMV and other social service agencies to reach the board. Therefore your registration will be postmarked timely as of the date received by the St. Lawrence County Board of Elections.

Requesting a Voter Registration Form Over the Phone

Call 315 379-2202 and a registration form will be sent to you in the mail to complete and return.

Register By Mail or e-Mail a Request for Application

Print an application form here complete and return through the mail; or, e-mail your request for a registration form to be sent to you. Be sure to include your mailing address. If you want to vote in an election, you must mail or deliver your completed registration form to the Board of Elections no later than 25 days before the election in which you want to vote. Your eligibility to vote will be based on the date you file this form, and the county board will notify you of such.

Registration Applications

Complete the entire application.

Remember to check the box next to the political party in which you wish to enroll. If you do not wish to be affiliated with any party, check the appropriate box.

Sign and date your application in blue or black ink. This is required!

Mail the completed application.

Send to: St. Lawrence County Board of Elections

80 State Highway 310, Canton, NY 13617

Re-Registering or Changing Your Information

Name, address or party enrollment changes can be made by submitting a new registration form in any manner permitted for a registration application. If you move, your notice of change of address must be received by the Board of Elections up to 20 days before the election in which you seek to vote. If you have missed the 20 day deadline, you may contact the Board of Elections for your new polling site.
